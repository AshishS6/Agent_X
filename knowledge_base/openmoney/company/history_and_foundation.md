# Open Money — History and Foundation

## Company Foundation

Open Money (also referred to as "OPEN" or "Open Financial Technologies") was established in **2017** with a clear mission to solve banking, payments, and accounting challenges faced by small and medium businesses in India.

## Founders

Open Money was founded by four entrepreneurs:

- **Anish Achuthan** — Co-founder
- **Mabel Chacko** — Co-founder
- **Ajeesh Achuthan** — Co-founder
- **Deena Jacob** — Co-founder

The company was born from the founders' personal experiences working closely with SMEs and recognizing the financial complexities they navigated daily without adequate tools or solutions.

## Mission

**To automate business finances, simplify payments, and improve cash flow management for SMEs and startups across India.**

The mission focuses on:
- Eliminating manual financial tasks
- Streamlining payment processes
- Providing better cash flow visibility
- Reducing reconciliation efforts
- Enabling businesses to focus on growth

## Vision

**To transform the way millions of businesses and accountants manage their business finances in partnership with leading national and international banks.**

The vision emphasizes:
- Scalable financial technology solutions
- Partnership with major banks
- Empowering SMEs and startups
- Supporting tax practitioners
- Building Asia's leading business payments platform

## Headquarters

**Location:** Bengaluru, Karnataka, India

Bengaluru serves as the company's headquarters and primary operational base, leveraging India's technology hub for talent and innovation.

## Company Identity

Open Money is:
- **Asia's first and largest neo-banking platform** designed specifically for SMEs, startups, and tax practitioners
- A **connected banking platform** that integrates with existing bank accounts
- A **business finance operating system** that orchestrates financial operations
- **Not a bank** — works in partnership with regulated banks
- **Not a payment gateway** — uses existing payment infrastructure

## Growth Journey
