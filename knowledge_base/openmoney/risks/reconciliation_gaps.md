# Reconciliation Gaps: What Happens Without Reconciliation

## Overview

**Without reconciliation, financial data in Open Money is provisional and may be inaccurate. Reconciliation gaps can cause significant financial errors and compliance issues.**

This is a critical risk that can lead to incorrect financial decisions and regulatory problems.

## What Are Reconciliation Gaps

Reconciliation gaps are:
- Unreconciled documents
- Unreconciled payments
- Unreconciled bank entries
- Mismatched records
- Missing links between records

**Reconciliation gaps mean financial data cannot be trusted.**

## Why Reconciliation Gaps Are Risky

### 1. Inaccurate Financial Data

**Risk:** Unreconciled data may be incorrect.

**Why it's risky:**
- Documents may show incorrect status
- Payments may not be matched
- Bank entries may be unmatched
- Amounts may not align

**Impact:**
- Users make decisions based on incorrect data
- Financial errors occur
- Compliance issues arise
- Audit problems occur

**Mitigation:**
- Reconcile regularly
- Verify all records
- Match all transactions
- Confirm data accuracy

### 2. Missing Payments

**Risk:** Payments may be received but not recorded.

**Why it's risky:**
- Payment received but not matched to document
- Document shows unpaid
- Outstanding amount is incorrect
- Financial records are inaccurate

**Impact:**
- Users think payment not received
- Users pursue incorrect collections
- Financial records are wrong
- Compliance issues occur

**Mitigation:**
- Reconcile regularly
- Match all payments to documents
- Verify payment status
- Confirm all payments are recorded

### 3. Duplicate Entries

**Risk:** Same payment may be recorded twice.

**Why it's risky:**
- Payment processed twice
- Double counting occurs
- Financial records are inflated
- Compliance issues arise

**Impact:**
- Users see incorrect amounts
- Financial records are wrong
- Compliance issues occur
- Audit problems occur

**Mitigation:**
- Reconcile regularly
- Identify duplicate entries
- Remove duplicates
- Verify data accuracy

### 4. Amount Mismatches

**Risk:** Amounts may not match between records.

**Why it's risky:**
- Document amount ≠ Payment amount
- Payment amount ≠ Bank entry amount
- Amounts don't align
- Financial records are inaccurate

**Impact:**
- Users see incorrect amounts
- Financial records are wrong
- Compliance issues occur
- Audit problems occur

**Mitigation:**
- Reconcile regularly
- Verify all amounts match
- Resolve mismatches
- Confirm data accuracy

## Mitigation Strategies

### 1. Reconcile Regularly

**Strategy:** Reconcile on a regular schedule.

**How:**
- Daily for active businesses
- Weekly for moderate activity
- Monthly for low activity
- Before financial reporting
- Before tax filing

### 2. Verify All Records

**Strategy:** Verify all records are reconciled.

**How:**
- Check reconciliation status
- Verify all documents are matched
- Verify all payments are matched
- Verify all bank entries are matched

### 3. Resolve Discrepancies

**Strategy:** Resolve all discrepancies immediately.

**How:**
- Identify discrepancies
- Investigate causes
- Fix issues
- Update records

### 4. Document Reconciliation

**Strategy:** Document all reconciliation activities.

**How:**
- Record reconciliation results
- Document discrepancies
- Track resolution actions
- Maintain audit trail

## Summary

- **Reconciliation gaps are risky** — Unreconciled data cannot be trusted
- **Gaps cause errors** — Inaccurate data leads to incorrect decisions
- **Gaps cause compliance issues** — Unreconciled data violates requirements
- **Reconcile regularly** — Prevent gaps through regular reconciliation
- **Verify all records** — Ensure all records are reconciled
- **Resolve discrepancies** — Fix issues immediately
- **Document reconciliation** — Maintain audit trail

**Bottom line:** Reconciliation gaps are dangerous. Reconcile regularly to prevent gaps. Never skip reconciliation. Always verify all records are reconciled.
