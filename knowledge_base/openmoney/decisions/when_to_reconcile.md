# When to Reconcile: Timing and Frequency

## Recommendation

**Reconcile daily for active businesses, weekly for moderate activity, and monthly for low activity. Always reconcile before financial reporting and tax filing.**

This is not just a suggestion—it's a mandatory requirement for financial accuracy.

## Reconciliation Frequency Guidelines

### Daily Reconciliation

**When:** For active businesses with high transaction volume.

**Why:**
- High transaction volume increases risk of discrepancies
- Early detection of issues
- Maintains accuracy for daily operations
- Prevents accumulation of discrepancies

**What to reconcile:**
- All transactions from previous day
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

**Time required:** 30-60 minutes per day

### Weekly Reconciliation

**When:** For moderate activity businesses.

**Why:**
- Regular verification without daily overhead
- Catches issues before they accumulate
- Maintains reasonable accuracy
- Manageable time commitment

**What to reconcile:**
- All transactions from previous week
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

**Time required:** 1-2 hours per week

### Monthly Reconciliation

**When:** For low activity businesses.

**Why:**
- Minimal transaction volume
- Periodic verification sufficient
- Compliance requirement
- Manageable for low activity

**What to reconcile:**
- All transactions from previous month
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

**Time required:** 2-4 hours per month

## Mandatory Reconciliation Times

### Before Financial Reporting

**When:** Before generating financial reports.

**Why:**
- Financial reports must be accurate
- Reports are used for decision-making
- Reports may be audited
- Accuracy is critical

**What to reconcile:**
- All transactions in reporting period
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

### Before Tax Filing

**When:** Before filing taxes.

**Why:**
- Tax filings must be accurate
- Tax authorities require accuracy
- Errors can cause penalties
- Compliance is mandatory

**What to reconcile:**
- All transactions in tax period
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

### After Discrepancy Detection

**When:** Immediately after detecting any discrepancy.

**Why:**
- Discrepancies indicate data issues
- Issues must be resolved quickly
- Prevents further problems
- Maintains accuracy

**What to reconcile:**
- All related transactions
- All payments and payouts
- All bank entries
- All documents

## What NOT to Do

### ❌ Don't Defer Reconciliation

**Wrong:** "I'll reconcile later when I have time."

**Correct:** "Reconciliation is mandatory. Schedule it regularly and don't defer."

### ❌ Don't Skip Reconciliation

**Wrong:** "Everything looks correct, so reconciliation is not needed."

**Correct:** "Reconciliation is mandatory regardless of appearance. Verify through reconciliation."

### ❌ Don't Reconcile Only When Problems Arise

**Wrong:** "I'll reconcile only when there's a problem."

**Correct:** "Reconcile regularly to prevent problems. Don't wait for issues to arise."

## Reconciliation Timing

### Best Time to Reconcile

**Recommended:** End of business day or week.

**Why:**
- All transactions are complete
- Bank statements are available
- Time to focus on reconciliation
- Prevents accumulation of work

### Worst Time to Reconcile

**Not recommended:** During busy periods or under time pressure.

**Why:**
- Rushed reconciliation leads to errors
- Incomplete reconciliation is worse than none
- Time pressure causes mistakes
- Better to schedule properly

## Reconciliation Checklist

Before starting reconciliation:

1. **Gather all data** — Documents, payments, bank statements
2. **Check sync status** — Ensure all data is synced
3. **Allocate time** — Set aside sufficient time
4. **Prepare tools** — Have reconciliation tools ready
5. **Clear distractions** — Focus on reconciliation

During reconciliation:

1. **Match all records** — Documents to payments to bank entries
2. **Verify all amounts** — Ensure amounts match
3. **Identify discrepancies** — Flag all mismatches
4. **Resolve issues** — Fix all discrepancies
5. **Confirm completion** — Verify all records are reconciled

After reconciliation:

1. **Document results** — Record reconciliation results
2. **Update status** — Mark records as reconciled
3. **Report issues** — Report any unresolved discrepancies
4. **Schedule next** — Schedule next reconciliation

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Infrequent Reconciliation

**Wrong:** "I'll reconcile once a quarter."

**Correct:** "Reconcile daily for active businesses, weekly for moderate, monthly for low activity."

### ❌ Mistake 2: Skipping Reconciliation

**Wrong:** "I'll skip reconciliation this week."

**Correct:** "Reconciliation is mandatory. Don't skip it."

### ❌ Mistake 3: Rushed Reconciliation

**Wrong:** "I'll quickly reconcile in 10 minutes."

**Correct:** "Allocate sufficient time for thorough reconciliation. Rushed reconciliation leads to errors."

## Summary

- **Daily:** For active businesses with high transaction volume
- **Weekly:** For moderate activity businesses
- **Monthly:** For low activity businesses
- **Before reporting:** Always reconcile before financial reporting
- **Before tax filing:** Always reconcile before tax filing
- **After discrepancies:** Reconcile immediately after detecting discrepancies

**Bottom line:** Reconcile regularly based on activity level. Never skip reconciliation. Always reconcile before critical reporting.
